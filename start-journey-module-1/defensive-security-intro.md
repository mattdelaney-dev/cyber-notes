# Defensive Security Intro

## Overview

This room provided a hands-on introduction to defensive security by protecting a simulated bank from an ongoing cyber attack.

Topics explored included:
- Thinking like a defender
- Detecting suspicious activity
- Identifying an active attack
- Stopping an attack in progress

## Key Concepts Learned

Defensive security focuses on protecting systems and data, detecting threats early, and responding effectively when an attack occurs. This room demonstrated those principles through a practical scenario involving a simulated financial institution.

---

## Thinking Like a Defender

Where an attacker looks for ways in, a defender asks "what would an attacker target, and how would I know if they were already here?"

A defensive mindset involves:
- Monitoring systems continuously for signs of compromise
- Understanding what normal behaviour looks like so that abnormal behaviour stands out
- Anticipating attacker techniques and preparing defences accordingly
- Responding quickly and methodically when an incident is detected

Defenders must be vigilant at all times because attacks can happen at any moment.

---

## Detecting Suspicious Activity

One of the core responsibilities of a defender is monitoring for unusual or suspicious behaviour across systems and networks.

Signs of suspicious activity can include:
- Login attempts from unusual locations or at unusual times
- A high volume of failed authentication attempts
- Unexpected outbound network connections
- Unusual processes running on a system
- Access to files or systems a user does not normally interact with

Security tools such as SIEM platforms, IDS, and log monitoring solutions help defenders surface these signals from large volumes of data.

---

## Identifying the Attack

Once suspicious activity is detected, defenders must investigate to understand what is happening.

This involves:
- Reviewing logs to trace the source and timeline of the activity
- Identifying which systems, accounts, or data may be affected
- Determining the attack technique being used
- Assessing how far the attacker has progressed

Accurate identification is critical because the response strategy depends on understanding the nature of the attack.

---

## Stopping the Attack

Once an attack is understood, defenders take action to contain and stop it.

Response actions can include:
- Blocking the attacker's IP address at the firewall
- Disabling compromised accounts
- Isolating affected systems from the network
- Terminating malicious processes
- Revoking unauthorised access

Speed is important during this phase. The longer an attacker remains in a system undetected, the more damage they can cause.

---

## Why This Matters

Defensive security is the foundation of how organisations protect themselves from cyber threats.

Defensive skills are applied in roles such as:
- SOC analyst
- Incident responder
- Threat hunter
- Security engineer
- Digital forensics investigator

Even organisations with strong preventive controls will eventually face an attack, making detection and response capabilities just as important as prevention.
